1. **AI can link fake online names to real identities in minutes for just a few dollars** (The Decoder)

        → This research from ETH Zurich and **Anthropic** demonstrating the de-anonymization of pseudonymous internet users using commercially available AI models is a watershed moment.         → The ongoing controversy surrounding **OpenAI's** and **Anthropic's** engagement with the Department of Defense highlights the complex ethical considerations surrounding AI development and deployment. While OpenAI attempts to build trust by publishing contract details, the vague "all lawful use" clause is proving to be a sticking point. This controversy is not just about optics; it's about the fundamental question of control and responsibility for AI's use in potentially harmful applications. Expect increased pressure on AI companies to adopt more transparent and restrictive policies regarding government contracts.
        
3. **Google looks to tackle longstanding RCS spam in India — but not alone** (TechCrunch)

        → **Google's** partnership with **Airtel** to combat RCS spam in India is a crucial step towards protecting users from malicious actors. India, with its massive mobile user base, has become a prime target for spam and fraud via RCS messaging. This initiative demonstrates Google's commitment to securing its platform and maintaining user trust, particularly in emerging markets. The success of this collaboration hinges on the effectiveness of the carrier-level filtering and Google's ability to adapt to evolving spam tactics. This move could set a precedent for similar partnerships in other regions facing similar challenges, and underscores the importance of collaboration between tech companies and telecommunication providers in combating online threats.
