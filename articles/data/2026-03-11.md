2. **An AI agent hacked McKinsey's internal AI platform in two hours using a decades-old technique** (The Decoder)

        Security firm **Codewall** demonstrated a critical vulnerability in **McKinsey's** internal AI platform, **Lilli**, by using an offensive AI agent to gain full read and write access to the production database in just two hours. The agent exploited a decades-old technique, highlighting the persistent challenge of securing AI systems against well-known attack vectors. This breach is particularly concerning given Lilli's widespread use by over 43,000 McKinsey employees for strategy work, client research, and document analysis, potentially exposing sensitive client data. The incident underscores the urgent need for robust security measures and continuous monitoring in AI-driven platforms, even those developed by leading consulting firms.
        → This event is a stark reminder that even sophisticated AI platforms are vulnerable to basic security flaws. Expect increased scrutiny on the security practices of AI platforms and a greater emphasis on proactive threat detection and mitigation strategies. It also highlights the potential risks associated with relying heavily on AI for sensitive business operations.

        Research & Breakthroughs
          
- **Prism-Δ**, a new method for prompt highlighting in large language models (LLMs), steers the model to prioritize user-specified text spans during generation. It decomposes the difference between positive and negative cross-covariance matrices to maximize discriminative energy while eliminating shared directions. The method matches or exceeds the best existing methods on 19 of 20 configurations, with relative gains up to +10.6%, while halving the fluency cost of steering. This allows for more precise control over LLM outputs, improving the relevance and accuracy of generated text.
- **CodePercept** research reveals that perception is the limiting factor in MLLMs' ability to perform STEM visual reasoning. The researchers constructed **ICC-1M**, a large-scale dataset comprising 1M Image-Caption-Code triplets, treating executable code as ground truth for visual perception. This approach enhances the perception capabilities of MLLMs by establishing code as a powerful perceptual medium, aligning with the structured nature of STEM visuals. This suggests that future improvements in STEM visual reasoning will require a greater focus on enhancing the perceptual abilities of MLLMs.
- **Just-in-Time (JiT)** is a novel training-free framework that accelerates Diffusion Transformers in the spatial domain. JiT formulates a spatially approximated generative ordinary differential equation (ODE) that drives the full latent state evolution based on computations from a dynamically selected, sparse subset of anchor tokens. This addresses the high computational cost of iterative sampling in Diffusion Transformers by exploiting the spatial redundancy inherent in the generative process. This could lead to more efficient and practical deployment of Diffusion Transformers for image synthesis.
