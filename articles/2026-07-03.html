    2A device that revives eyeballs from dead donors could make eye transplants possible (MIT Tech Review)

    The development of a device that can revive eyeballs from dead donors is a groundbreaking achievement in medical research. This innovation has the potential to make eye transplants possible, addressing a significant challenge in ophthalmology. The success of such a device would rely on the ability to maintain the viability of the eyeball outside the body, a feat that has eluded surgeons until now. This breakthrough matters because it could restore vision to individuals who have lost their sight due to corneal damage or other eye diseases. As researchers continue to refine this technology, we can expect significant advancements in the field of ophthalmology, potentially leading to improved outcomes for patients awaiting eye transplants.
