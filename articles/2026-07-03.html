    2A device that revives eyeballs from dead donors could make eye transplants possible (MIT Tech Review)

    Researchers have made a groundbreaking discovery with a device that can revive eyeballs from dead donors, potentially making eye transplants possible. This development is crucial because it addresses a significant challenge in eye transplantation: the rapid degeneration of eyeballs after they are removed from the body. The device's ability to preserve the viability of donated eyeballs could revolutionize the field of ophthalmology and offer hope to individuals suffering from vision loss. As this technology advances, it will be essential to consider the ethical and regulatory implications of using deceased donor eyeballs for transplantation. The success of this device could also pave the way for similar innovations in other areas of organ transplantation.

    3AI’s Volatile Power Use Quietly Tests Grid Limits (IEEE Spectrum)

    The rapid expansion of AI infrastructure is quietly testing the limits of the power grid, with data centers projected to consume a growing share of global electricity demand. This issue is critical because the volatile power use of AI systems can strain the grid and lead to instability. As the demand for AI computing continues to grow, it is essential to develop more efficient and sustainable power solutions to support the industry's expansion. The International Energy Agency's estimate that data centers could account for 3 to 4 percent of total global electricity consumption highlights the need for AI companies to prioritize energy efficiency and invest in renewable energy sources.
